3. Noise in the foyer: We agreed that it is a matter of greeter disrcretion whether to ask someone to wait in the library or let them go into Meeting. But some suggestions:

  • If someone is going into the Meeting late, then holding the door for them allows them to focus on finding a place and you can ensure that the door closes quietly.
  • Make sure the door between library and Meeting room is closed. And closing it can signal to those in the library that Meeting has begun.
  • It is better to ask Friends to please take their conversation outside rather than asking them to be quiet.
  • And, asking someone you know who won't be offended can serve as a signal to others.
  • Ringing the bell again even though it has already been rung can at least assure members in Meeting that the greeter is trying to achieve silence even if the effort doesn't succeed.
  • An announcement might be made at MWAB about the need for silence--but not after worship as there is often a surfeit of announcements.

4. Newcomer teas: We are at unity that newcomer teas are desirable and that the Hospitality Committee should resume this role perhaps every six months. One idea is to sponsor a light luncheon in the schoolhouse after Meeting.
