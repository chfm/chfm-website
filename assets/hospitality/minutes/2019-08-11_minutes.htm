2. What is Hospitality's role at memorial services?
The committee came to unity on the following statement for inclusion in the document on celebrations to be posted on the Meeting website:

Care and Counsel and a representative from the Hospitality Committee will work with the family on planning any reception following the memorial service if it is held on the grounds of the meeting. Volunteers in the community may be asked to provide drinks and light refreshments.

And Jennifer very generously volunteered to be the representative from Hospitality.

3.Planning for the coming year:

Lotsa Helping Hands:

  • First of all there needs to be greater coordination between C&C and Hospitality.
  • Because we have never used Lotsa Helping Hands since establishing our group there, it is desirable to do a test run. I will add all the members of Hospitality to Lotsa Helping Hands (no, this doesn't commit you to providing meals ;-) and send a trial request to which everyone can respond.
  • If we are to continue using Lotsa Helping Hands we need more potential volunteers than the 10 who signed up 2 years ago.

Refreshments at Fellowship:

  • Over the past several years the committee has made an effort to provide higher quality refreshments to facilitate and enhance fellowship. .
  • Issues were raised to keep in mind as we move forward: how to pay for refreshments (although the committee encourages members to submit their expenses, most have not), how much refreshments to provide, and whether they make a worthwhile contribution.

Contacting Visitors:

  • It is very impactful for a Friend to meet one-on-one with a visitor who is open to that, so it would be good if we can compile a list of Friends who are willing to do so.
  • A mailing list is probably sufficient for the purpose.
  • Since Hart has undertaken the responsibility of monitoring the guestbook and mailing a personal note to visitors who leave their mailing address, it is important that she participate in any upcoming discussion.

Visitors' Tea:

  • In the past, three times year, Hospitality has hosted a tea at the Meeting house for recent visitors following worship. This is something we can consider reviving -- with frequency and other details to be discussed.

Thanksgiving:

  • We know we don't want to solicit guests to be hosted at Thanksgiving when we have no prospective hosts to receive them. What we do want to do remains to be decided.

Thank you, Friends.
